About

Learning circles began at Chicago Public Library in 2015, and have since spread to more than 40 library systems around the world. Given the success of last years gathering in Kansas City, we’ve decided to host another event this year.

Goals

You can’t run a learning circle without setting goals, right? Together, our meeting aims to achieve the following:

  • Get to know one another
  • Share and document stories, outcomes, and best practices for supporting learning circles within libraries and community centers
  • Co-design new exercises and tools that support learning circles and other related library programs
  • Explore and build consensus about the learning circle model, its values, and potential directions for our movement

Getting to Boston

Boston Logan airport (BOS) is located very close to the city, with bus, train, and rideshare connections to the surrounding areas.

Who will be there

We welcome attendance from all learning circle practitioners, facilitators, P2PU friends and supporters, librarians and educators, and those committed to peer, community-based, equitable and open learning. Our aim is for this to be an intimate and interactive event with about 50 participants.

We are grateful to the Institute of Museum and Library Services for their support in making this event possible.

Registration

If you would like to attend, we would love to have you. Our only expectation is that those who attend will actively contribute their experience and have an interest in the development of learning circles and P2PU. There is no big registration process necessary and no registration fee. The only costs you incur are travel to Boston, lodging, and possibly some food expenses.

FAQ

How much does it cost to attend?

There are no fees to attend the conference. Getting yourself to Boston and paying for accomodation and some food will be your main costs.

Can P2PU help cover my travel costs to Boston?

Although we don’t have any funds on hand to support your travel (we wish we did), we’ve worked with a number of participants to help advocate for funding from their organizations. We’re happy to provide more details about the event if it helps you find the money to come.

Will the event be live streamed or recorded?

We plan to record as much as we can and share what we learned with everyone else through photos, short videos, and a written summary. However, there won’t be hour long recorded lectures so that you can “learn online” at home. That’s not really our style.

I’ve never run or supported a learning circle or P2PU before, can I still come?

Maybe. We’re open to those who are truly interested in our collective work. Send Nico a message and let us know why you’re interested in coming (nico@p2pu.org).
